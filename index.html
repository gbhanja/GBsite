Research Profile

My research interest lies at the intersection of quantum information theory and quantum thermodynamics, exploring the fundamental limits of work extraction and quantum energy storage. A central focus of my work is characterising ergotropy—the maximum amount of work that can be unitarily extracted from a quantum state—particularly within the rich, infinite-dimensional phase space of continuous-variable (CV) systems. By investigating the complex interplay between robust quantum correlations, tailored dissipation, and non-Markovian memory effects, I aim to understand how environment-induced dynamics can be controlled or leveraged to design highly efficient, scalable quantum batteries.

I enjoy combining analytical techniques with numerical simulations to investigate the fundamental limits of quantum technologies.

Away from the demands of academia, my mind oscillates between the deliberate foresight of chess and the spirited momentum of cricket. When the time comes to truly unplug, an empty whiteboard becomes my favorite retreat—an open horizon where idle scribbles and wandering ideas are allowed to beautifully unfold without a destination.
